# 14.06 Foreign Group ACL Principals Intra-Forest Child→Parent

> Hunt cross-domain group membership (`Get-DomainForeignUser`) and ACLs, then abuse via `Rubeus createnetonly` sacrificial sessions.

## Overview

Users from a child domain may be members of Universal or Domain Local groups in the parent domain (foreign group membership) or hold ACL rights (GenericAll, GenericWrite, etc.) on objects in the parent domain. Enumerate with Get-DomainForeignUser and Get-DomainObjectAcl, then use Rubeus createnetonly to create a sacrificial session and abuse the cross-domain privilege.

## Steps

- **Get-DomainForeignUser** — enumerate child domain users who are members of parent domain groups
- **Get-DomainGroup -Identity ... | select memberof** — check what built-in groups the foreign group belongs to
- **Get-DomainObjectAcl** with SID filter — find child domain users holding ACL rights in parent domain
- **ConvertFrom-SID** to resolve SID values to usernames
- **Rubeus createnetonly** — create sacrificial logon session to avoid ticket conflicts
- **Rubeus asktgt** in that session, then abuse via **New-DomainUser**, **Add-DomainGroupMember**, **Set-DomainUserPassword**, or **Whisker**
